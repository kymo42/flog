FLOG

Simple GPS Golf Rangefinder for Fitbit

FLOG is a super simple, "no-fuss" rangefinder for your Fitbit Versa 3 or Fitbit Sense. It handles the distances so you can focus on your swing. It works on ANY golf course in the world because you teach it where the greens are as you play!

üöÄ Quick Start (In 3 Simple Steps)

1. Start a Round
On your watch, tap NEW COURSE. The watch will start looking for your location (GPS).
2. Jump to any Hole
Tapping the "HOLE X" text at the top of the main screen now opens a grid of all 18 holes. This allows you to jump to any hole instantly to see distances or mark a new green.
3. "Mark" Your Greens
The first time you play a course, walk to the middle of the green on any hole.
  • Tap the red EDIT button in the top corner (this "unlocks" the marking button).
  • Tap the yellow MARK button.
  • Tap YES, SET HERE. (Note: If you have no GPS signal, the button will show "WAITING..." - simply wait for a better view of the sky).
  • Your watch vibrates. Done! Now the watch knows exactly where that green is.
  • Tap EDIT again to lock it (Back to "Play Mode").
4. Just Play
Next time you stand on the fairway, the watch will show you exactly how many Meters (or Yards) you are from the center of that green.

üì± How to Name Your Course

To give your round a real name (like Pebble Beach):

  1. Open the Fitbit App on your phone.
  2. Tap your Watch Icon (top left).
  3. Tap Gallery → Flog → Settings.
  4. Type the name in the "Course Name" box.
  5. Your watch will update instantly!

ü§ù How to Share with Friends

Did you map a perfect course? Share it with your buddies!

  • Go to the Flog Settings on your phone (see above).
  • Copy the long Export Code and text it to your friend.
  • They paste it into their "Import Code" box.
  • Their watch now has all your markers!

üí° Pro Tips for Best Results
  • Battery Safe: The screen will turn off or dim normally to save battery. Just lift your wrist (gesture) to wake it up and see the latest distance.
  • Wait for GPS: Stand outside for 30 seconds when you first start. numbers will show "---" until the watch connects.
  • Play Mode vs Edit Mode: Keep it in Play Mode (locked) most of the time to prevent accidental changes.
  • Battery: GPS uses power. Start with at least 50% charge for a full 18 holes.
